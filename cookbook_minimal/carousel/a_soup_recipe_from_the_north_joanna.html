A soup recipe from the North

by Joanna

Here is a tasty soup, just in time for summer when you need it. ;)

Ingredients

  Quantity
Onion 1 Bulb
Carrot 2 Fruits
Root celery 1 piece
Garlic x cloves (you decide)
Yellow pepper Maybe half a Fruit
Crème fraîche A few table spoons
Potato 4 small Fruit
Sweet potato 1 Fruit
Red lentils 1 Cup
Chopped tomatoes 1 Can
other ingredients Tomato purée, Lemon juice, Olive oil,
Fresh or frozen Parsley, Chilli flakes,
Paprika powder, Salt, Black pepper

Instructions in 4 steps

Step 1

Basically chop onion and garlic, then potatoes (smallish pieces), both sweet and normal, as well as the peeled root celery, yellow pepper and carrots.

Step 2

Add the onion and then the garlics to a pot/deep frying pan with heated olive oil. After a couple of minutes when these are soft and shiny, add rest of the chopped stuff, fry it all together in the same pot/pan with salt and the spices. add it to a pot/deep frying pan with heated olive oil. After a couple of minutes when these are soft and shiny.

Step 3

After a little while, add a few squeezes of tomato purée and add some water (just a little, like a cup). Let it all simmer for a couple of minutes and then add the can of chopped tomatoes. Turn down the heat so that the soup keeps its juiciness - you might want to add some water if too much boils off.

Step 4

After a while, add the red lentils too. Let it all simmer and cook until all the veggies and lentils are ready. During this time you can add more spices if needed, and lemon juice. When the soup has a nice texture and is ready, and you’re happy with the seasoning, add a big pinch of fresh or frozen parsley, and stir. Also add a few table spoons of creme fraiche and stir. Ready!

Best served with a slice of sourdough bread served with salty butter and a slice of Swedish cheese. :)
